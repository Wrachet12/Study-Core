Mind map — one-pager for a topic

Double-click anywhere to drop a bubble. Drag bubbles around, click the text to edit it. Turn on Connect, then click two bubbles in a row to draw a line between them; click the same pair again to remove that line.

Leitner box system — hardest → easiest

Add a concept card to whichever box fits. Reviews only unlock once a card's interval has actually passed — that's what keeps the XP honest.

Flashcards

5 stacks, up to 150 cards each. Rename a stack to match a subject, then add cards and flip through them.

Feynman Technique

Explain a concept so simply a beginner could follow it. The gaps you find are exactly what to restudy. Use the worksheet in the Notes tab.

Parkinson's Law

Work expands to fill the time you give it. In the Planner tab you can tighten a deadline on purpose to create healthy urgency.

Pomodoro Technique

Short focused sprints followed by a short break keep attention fresh. Run it from the Timer tab.

Add an assignment

Tip: write the next concrete step, not the finish line. "Study this topic for the test" is risky — break it into named steps like "Outline causes of WWI" or "Redo missed practice problems".
